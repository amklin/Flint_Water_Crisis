TIMELINE

2011

Flint Emergency Manager Appointed

Michigan Governor Rick Snyder appoints an emergency manager to cut costs. At the time, Flint had a $25 million deficit due to decades-long issues with the declining auto industry.

Apr 16, 2013

Announcement of Water System Switch

Flint Emergency Manager Ed Kurtz announces that the city is going to switch from Detroit Water and Sewerage Department (DWSD) and join the Karegnondi Water Authority (KWA). This is supposed to save the city $200 million over 25 years.

Apr 2014

Changes in Water Quality

As a temporary cost-cutting measure, Flint starts taking water from the Flint River. The poorly-treated water corroded the lead pipes and contaminated the water. Residents immediately complained about water taste, smell, and color, and reported rashes and hair loss.

Jan 2015

Flint Refuses to Switch Water Source

Even after Detroit offers its own water systems, Flint leaders insist their water is safe enough.

Sept 24, 2015

Doctor Recommendations Ignored

Doctors find high levels of lead in children's blood and urge Flint to stop using the Flint River for water. However, state regulators continue to insist that the water is safe.

Sept 29, 2015

Governor Promises Action

Then-Governor Rick Snyder promises to take action in reponse to the high lead levels. This is the first time the state acknowledged there was a problem.

Aug 20, 2015

Covering Things Up

In the initial report on lead levels by the Michigan Department of Environmental Quality (MDEQ), two samples were dropped. This put the result of the report back into the federally required levels of lead.

Oct 16, 2015

Switching back to Detroit's Water System

Then-Governor Snyder announces $1 million dedicated to buying testing water in Flint public schools and buying water filters. Days later, Flint switches back their water supply back to Detroit Water and Sewage Department (DWSD).

Dec 14,2015

Mayor Declares State of Emergency

Flint Mayor Karen Weaver declares a state of emergency.
