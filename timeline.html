TIMELINE

2011

Flint Emergency Manager Appointed

Michigan Governor Rick Snyder appoints an emergency manager to cut costs. At the time, Flint had a $25 million deficit due to decades-long issues with the declining auto industry.

🢓
Apr 16, 2013

Announcement of Water System Switch

Flint Emergency Manager Ed Kurtz announces that the city is going to switch from Detroit Water and Sewerage Department (DWSD) and join the Karegnondi Water Authority (KWA). This is supposed to save the city $200 million over 25 years.

🢑
🢓
Apr 2014

Changes in Water Quality

As a temporary cost-cutting measure, Flint starts taking water from the Flint River. The poorly-treated water corrodes the lead pipes, which contaminates the water. Residents immediately complain about water taste, smell, and color, and begin reporting rashes and hair loss.

Leaders ignore this. The Flint Department of Public Works Director Howard Croft is even quoted as saying, "The test results have shown that our water is not only safe, but of the high quality that Flint customers have come to expect. We are proud of that end result."

🢑
🢓
Jan 2015

Flint Refuses to Switch Water Source

Even after Detroit offers its own water systems, Flint leaders insist their water is safe enough.

🢑
🢓
Sept 24, 2015

Ignoring Doctor Recommendations

A group of doctors find high levels of lead in children’s blood and urge Flint to stop using the Flint River as a water source. The first doctor to uncover this issue is Dr. Hanna-Attisha. In 2018, she spoke about her experiences in a podcast with NPR. You can listen below.

Despite recommendations by doctors like Dr. Hanna-Attisha, state regulators continue insisting the water is safe, essentially silencing them.

🢑
🢓
Sept 29, 2015

Governor Promises Action

Then-Governor Rick Snyder promises to take action in response to the high lead levels.

🢑
🢓
Aug 20, 2015

Cover Up

In the initial report on lead levels by the Michigan Department of Environmental Quality (MDEQ), two samples are dropped, which puts the result back within the federally required levels of lead.

🢑
🢓
Oct 16, 2015

Switching Back to Detroit's Water System

Then-Governor Snyder announces $1 million dedicated to buying water filters and testing water in Flint public schools. Days later, Flint switches back to Detroit’s water system.

🢑
🢓
Dec 14, 2015

City State of Emergency

Flint Mayor Karen Weaver declares a state of emergency.

🢑
🢓
Dec 29, 2015

Resignation of Department of Environmental Quality Director

The state’s Department of Environmental Quality Director Dan Wyant resigns and apologizes for what happened in Flint.

🢑
🢓
Jan 5, 2016

Governor Declares State of Emergency

Governor Rick Snyder declares a state of emergency. A week later, the Michigan National Guard starts helping distribute bottled water and filters.

🢑
🢓
Mar 23, 2016

Michigan is "Fundamentally Accountable"

A governor-appointed panel concludes that Michigan is "fundamentally accountable" for the crisis due to decisions made by environmental regulators.

🢑
🢓
April-July 2016

Holding Officials Accountable

Criminal charges are filed against several officials. You can watch the video to find out more about the court case and the citizens' attempts to get justice.

🢑
🢓
Dec 20, 2016

Charged with Felonies

Two Flint emergency managers and two water plant officials are charged with felonies.

🢑
🢓
June 2017

Resignation of Department of Environmental Quality Director

Five officials, including Michigan Health Department Director Nick Lyon, are charged with involuntary manslaughter. This follows two deaths from Legionnaire’s Disease caused by the water crisis. Officials are accused of covering up the crisis, with Lyon accused of failing to inform public and Dr. Eden Wells accused of threatening to withhold funding from researchers who had started investigating the outbreak.

🢑
🢓
May 2019

Confiscation of Evidence

Snyder’s hard drive and cell phone are confiscated for investigation.

🢑
🢓
Jan 13 2021

Willful Neglect of Duty

Snyder and the ex-Director of Flint Public Works Howard Croft are each charged with willful neglect of duty.
