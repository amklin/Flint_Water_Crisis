TIMELINE

Poor Practices and Decisions

2011

Flint Emergency Manager Appointed

Michigan Governor Rick Snyder appoints an emergency manager to cut costs. At the time, Flint had a $25 million deficit due to decades-long issues with the declining auto industry.

Apr 16, 2013

Announcement of Water System Switch

Flint Emergency Manager Ed Kurtz announces that the city is going to switch from Detroit Water and Sewerage Department (DWSD) and join the Karegnondi Water Authority (KWA). This is supposed to save the city $200 million over 25 years.

April 2014

Changes in Water Quality

As a temporary cost-cutting measure, Flint starts taking water from the Flint River. The poorly-treated water corroded the lead pipes and contaminated the water. Residents immediately complained about water taste, smell, and color, and reported rashes and hair loss.

January 2015

Flint Refuses to Switch Water Source

Even after Detroit offers its own water systems, Flint leaders insist their water is safe enough.

September 24, 2015

Ignoring Doctor Recommendations

A group of doctors found high levels of lead in children’s blood and urged Flint to stop using the Flint River as a water source. However, state regulators continued insisting the water was safe.

September 29, 2015

Governor Promises Action

Then-Governor Rick Snyder promised to take action to respond to the high lead levels.

August 20, 2015

Cover Up

In the Michigan's Department of Environmental Quality (MDEQ) initial report on lead levels, two samples were dropped, which put the result back into the federally required levels of lead.

Finally Taking Action

October 16, 2015

Switching Back to Detroit's Water System

Then-Governor Snyder announces $1 million dedicated to buying water filters and testing water in Flint public schools. Days later, Flint to switches back to Detroit’s water system.

December 14, 2015

City State of Emergency

Mayor Karen Weaver declares a state of emergency.

December 29, 2015

Resignation of Department of Environmental Quality Director

The state’s Department of Environmental Quality Director Dan Wyant resigns and apologizes for what happened in Flint.

January 5, 2016

Governor Declares State of Emergency

Governor Rick Snyder declare a state of emergency. A week later, the Michigan National Guard starts helping distribute bottled water and filters.

January 16, 2016

President Declares State of Emergency

Then-President Obama declares a state of emergency and provides aid. Then-Attorney General of Michigan, Bill Schuette, begins an "independent review".

March 23, 2016

Michigan is "Fundamentally Responsible"

A governor-appointed panel concludes that Michigan is "fundamentally accountable" for the crisis due to decisions made by environmental regulators.



Holding Officials Accountable

April-July 2016

Criminal Charges

Criminal charges are filed against several officials.

December 20, 2016

Charged with Felonies

Two Flint emergency managers and two water plant officials are charged with felonies.

June 2017

Resignation of Department of Environmental Quality Director

Five officials, including Michigan Health Department Director Nick Lyon, were charged with involuntary manslaughter. This followed two deaths from Legionnaire’s Disease caused by the water crisis. Officials were accused of covering up to the crisis, with Lyon accused of failing to inform public and Dr. Eden Wells accused of threatening to withhold funding from researchers who had started investigating the outbreak.
