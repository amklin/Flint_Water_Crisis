SOLUTIONS

Most Permanent Solution: Replacing Pipes

The city can replace all the lead pipes with plastic pipes and replace the contaminated water with clean water.

Experts estimate that doing this would cost at least $1.5 billion, making it the most expensive solution, as well.

However, this would be worth it to ensure the safety of the thousands of Flint citizens, and it is the most permanent solution to the crisis.

Alternate Solutions

❮

❯

Water Bottles

An immediate measure that can be taken is switching to water bottles for obtaining and consuming water, ensuring its high quality.

However, due to the sheer amount of water that would be needed for various home amenities (e.g., showers, sprinklers, toilets), this would not be sustainable in the long term.

However, this could be a good measure to take while the government is replacing pipes.

Home Filtration

Individuals and families can install filtration systems into their own homes.

The EPA and CDC have already conducted studies and determined that filtered water is safe to drink. In fact, they have declared that lead levels after filtration are "too low to be tested."

Thus, this would remove heavy metals from water, allowing people to use it.

This solution would give people control over their own water and would limit costs to the city government.

However, this may end up being more expensive overall as each household would have to pay for these systems. Therefore, while it may not be sustainable in the long term, this could be a temporary solution while the government takes more drastic steps.

Legislative Measures

The city government could implement a variety of legislative measures to limit the damage continuing to be done and to ensure a crisis like this doesn't happen again.

In particular, they should emphasize water quality in policy decisions.

To prevent further worsening of water quality, they could outlaw the dumping of water in waterways, especially those used for human consumption.
